# Revenue and User Retention Analytics

## Overview

This project analyzes user retention behavior and its impact on business revenue. The objective is to understand how customer engagement, retention, and churn influence long-term business growth and profitability.

By examining user activity and revenue metrics, the project provides insights into customer behavior and identifies opportunities to improve retention and maximize customer value.

---

## Business Problem

Acquiring new customers is often more expensive than retaining existing ones. Businesses need to understand:

* How many users continue using the product?
* How many users stop using the product?
* How does retention affect revenue?
* Which user groups contribute most to long-term growth?

The goal of this project is to analyze retention patterns and evaluate their impact on revenue generation.

---

## Objectives

* Measure user retention and churn rates.
* Analyze revenue trends across different user cohorts.
* Identify relationships between retention and revenue growth.
* Generate actionable business insights to improve customer engagement.

---

## Key Metrics

### Retention Rate

Percentage of users who continue using the product after a given period.

### Churn Rate

Percentage of users who stop using the product.

### Revenue

Total income generated from customers.

### Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

Estimated revenue generated by a customer throughout their relationship with the business.

---

## Methodology

1. Data Collection and Cleaning
2. User Activity Analysis
3. Retention and Churn Calculation
4. Revenue Analysis
5. Customer Segmentation
6. Business Insight Generation

---

## Key Insights

* Higher retention rates are strongly associated with increased revenue.
* Returning customers contribute significantly more value than one-time users.
* Small improvements in retention can generate substantial long-term revenue growth.
* Identifying churn patterns helps businesses take proactive retention measures.

---

## Business Impact

The analysis demonstrates how improving customer retention can:

* Increase recurring revenue
* Reduce customer acquisition costs
* Improve customer lifetime value
* Enhance long-term business profitability
